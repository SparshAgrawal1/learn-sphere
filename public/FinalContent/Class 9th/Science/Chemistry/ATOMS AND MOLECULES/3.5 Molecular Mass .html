The molecular mass of a substance is the sum of the atomic masses of all atoms in a molecule. It represents the relative mass of a molecule expressed in atomic mass units (u). For ionic compounds, we use the term "formula unit mass" instead.

‚ö° Formula Unit Mass
For ionic compounds like sodium chloride (NaCl), we use the term "formula unit mass" instead of molecular mass. The calculation method is the same - we add the atomic masses of all atoms in the formula unit. For example, NaCl has a formula unit mass of 23 + 35.5 = 58.5 u
